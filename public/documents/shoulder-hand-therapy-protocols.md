### Clinical Context
- **Primary Issue:** SICK Scapula secondary to rib instability
- **Mechanism:** Loss of stable rib platform affects scapular positioning
- **Pattern:** Forward tilt (Pec Minor dominance), winging, dyskinesis
- **Principle:** "Treat the ribs to fix the shoulder"

---

### Shoulder Assessment Protocol

#### Visual Assessment
**Standing Posture Analysis:**
1. **Front View:**
   - Shoulder height symmetry
   - Clavicle angle
   - AC joint prominence

2. **Side View:**
   - Forward head position
   - Rounded shoulders
   - Scapular tilt

3. **Back View:**
   - Scapular winging
   - Medial border prominence
   - Inferior angle position

#### Movement Testing
**Test 1: Wall Angel**
- Back against wall
- Arms in "goal post" position
- Slide up and down
- Note: Can't keep contact? Restriction location?

**Test 2: Scapular Wall Test**
- Stand arm's length from wall
- Push-up position against wall
- Note: Winging? Asymmetry?

**Test 3: Empty Can Test**
- Arms at 30Â° forward
- Thumbs down
- Lift against resistance
- Note: Weakness? Pain?

### Shoulder Stabilization Program

#### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

**1. Scapular Setting**
*Position:* Sitting or standing
*Technique:*
- Gentle squeeze shoulder blades together
- Down and back motion
- Hold 5 seconds
- NO forcing or pain
*Sets:* 10 reps, 3x daily
*Cue:* "Put shoulder blades in back pockets"

**2. Wall Slides**
*Position:* Back against wall
*Technique:*
- Arms at 90Â° against wall
- Slide up maintaining contact
- Stop before losing contact
- Slowly return
*Sets:* 10 reps, 2x daily
*Progress:* Increase range weekly

**3. Prone T's and Y's**
*Position:* Prone on bed, arms hanging
*Technique:*
- T: Arms out to side, thumbs up
- Y: Arms at 45Â° forward, thumbs up
- Lift arms parallel to floor
- Hold 3 seconds
*Sets:* 10 each, 1x daily
*Modification:* Start with no weight

**4. Serratus Punch**
*Position:* Supine, arm straight up
*Technique:*
- Punch ceiling without bending elbow
- Feel shoulder blade glide on ribs
- Control return
*Sets:* 15 reps, 2x daily
*Note:* Critical for rib-scapula connection

#### Phase 2: Strengthening (Weeks 5-8)

**1. Resistance Band Rows**
- Multiple angles (high, mid, low)
- Focus on scapular motion
- 3x12 each angle

**2. External Rotation**
- Elbow at side
- Towel under arm
- Band resistance
- 3x15 each arm

**3. Face Pulls**
- Band at face height
- Pull to face, elbows high
- Emphasize rear delts
- 3x15

**4. Bear Crawl Hold**
- Quadruped position
- Knees 1 inch off ground
- Hold 30 seconds
- 3 sets

#### Phase 3: Integration (Weeks 9-12)

**1. Turkish Get-Up Components**
- Break down full movement
- Practice each transition
- Add light weight

**2. Bottoms-Up Kettlebell Carry**
- Light weight
- Overhead position
- Walk slowly
- Switch arms

**3. Cable/Band PNF Patterns**
- Diagonal patterns
- Incorporate rotation
- Full range of motion

### Shoulder-Rib Integration Exercises

**1. Rib Roll with Reach**
*Position:* Side-lying on right
*Technique:*
- Left arm reaches overhead
- Feel ribs expand
- Add gentle rib mobilization
- Coordinate with breathing
*Purpose:* Links shoulder motion to rib expansion

**2. Quadruped Rib-Scap Rocks**
*Position:* Hands and knees
*Technique:*
- Rock forward/back
- Focus on scapular glide
- Maintain neutral spine
*Purpose:* Retrains scapular-thoracic rhythm

**3. Wall Corner Stretch with Breathing**
*Position:* Facing corner
*Technique:*
- Hands on walls
- Step in to stretch pecs
- Deep breathing into ribs
- 5 breaths, 3 sets
*Purpose:* Releases pec minor while mobilizing ribs

## âœ‹ Part 2: Hand Therapy Protocols

### Hand-Rib Connection
- Grip strength correlates with core stability
- Hand function affects entire kinetic chain
- Fine motor control improves with proximal stability

---

### Hand Assessment

#### Range of Motion Testing
**Measure and Document:**
1. Finger flexion (to palm)
2. Finger extension
3. Thumb opposition
4. Wrist flexion/extension
5. Forearm pronation/supination

#### Strength Testing
1. Grip dynamometer (if available)
2. Pinch strength (lateral, tip, palmar)
3. Individual finger strength

#### Functional Testing
1. Button manipulation
2. Coin pickup
3. Writing sample
4. Jar opening

---

### Hand Therapy Exercise Program

#### Warm-Up Routine (5 minutes)

**1. Tendon Glides**
- Start: Fingers straight
- Position 1: Hook fist
- Position 2: Full fist
- Position 3: Straight fist
- Position 4: Return to straight
*Reps:* 10 cycles

**2. Nerve Glides**
*Median Nerve:*
- Arm out, palm up
- Extend wrist and fingers
- Turn head away
- Hold 3 seconds
*Reps:* 10 each arm

**3. Wrist Circles**
- Slow, controlled circles
- 10 clockwise
- 10 counterclockwise
- Each wrist

---

### Strengthening Program

#### Week 1-2: Foundation

**1. Therapy Putty Exercises**
*Gripping:*
- Squeeze putty in palm
- Hold 5 seconds
- 15 reps, 3 sets

*Finger Pinch:*
- Pinch putty between fingers and thumb
- Each finger individually
- 10 reps per finger

*Finger Spread:*
- Wrap putty around fingers
- Spread fingers apart
- 10 reps, 3 sets

**2. Rubber Band Extensions**
- Band around all fingers
- Spread fingers against resistance
- Hold 3 seconds
- 15 reps, 3 sets

**3. Towel Wringing**
- Wring towel in both directions
- 30 seconds each direction
- 3 sets

#### Week 3-4: Progression

**1. Weighted Wrist Curls**
- Light dumbbell (1-3 lbs)
- Palm up and palm down
- 12 reps each, 3 sets

**2. Finger Walking**
- Walk fingers up wall
- Return under control
- 10 trips, 2 sets

**3. Ball Squeezes**
- Tennis ball or stress ball
- Sustained squeeze 5 seconds
- Quick pulses 20 reps
- 3 sets each

#### Week 5-8: Advanced

**1. Rice Bucket Exercises**
- Bury hand in rice
- Open/close fist
- Twist and grab
- 2 minutes, 3 sets

**2. Finger Independence**
- Piano exercises on table
- Isolate each finger
- Speed and accuracy
- 5 minutes daily

**3. Grip Variations**
- Farmer's carry with light weight
- Pinch grip plates
- Finger tip push-ups (modified)

### Hand Therapy for Rib Support

#### Concept: Irradiation
When hands work hard, core activates automatically

**Exercise 1: Plank Plus**
- Plank position
- Make fists and grip hard
- Feel core engage more
- Hold 20 seconds

**Exercise 2: Farmer's Walk with Breath**
- Light weights in hands
- Walk slowly
- Coordinate with breathing
- 30 seconds

**Exercise 3: Dead Hang**
- Hang from bar (if able)
- Engage grip fully
- Feel entire chain activate
- Build to 30 seconds

### Integration Exercises

#### Full Chain Patterns

**1. Reach, Grab, Pull**
*Setup:* Resistance band anchored high
*Action:*
- Reach up with both hands
- Grab band firmly
- Pull down to chest
- Feel connection from hands to ribs
*Sets:* 12 reps, 3 sets

**2. Push-Pull Alternation**
*Setup:* Two bands, different heights
*Action:*
- Push with right while pulling with left
- Switch smoothly
- Maintain rib stability
*Sets:* 10 alternations, 3 sets

**3. Carrying Variations**
- Suitcase carry (one side)
- Farmer's walk (both sides)
- Overhead carry
- Mixed carry (one up, one down)
*Distance:* 30 yards each

- Wrist neutral position
- Mouse close to keyboard

### Common Problems & Solutions

#### Problem: Morning Stiffness
**Solution:**
- Sleep in resting splints
- Morning warm water soak
- Gentle movement before activity

#### Problem: Fatigue with Writing
**Solution:**
- Larger grip pens
- Frequent breaks
- Alternate hands when possible
- Support forearm

#### Problem: Weakness with Gripping
**Solution:**
- Progressive putty program
- Isometric holds
- Gradual loading
- Check shoulder stability

### Safety Considerations

#### Red Flags
- Sudden loss of function
- Color changes in fingers
- Severe pain with exercise
- Numbness/tingling spreading

#### Modifications for Rib Protection
- Avoid exercises causing rib pain
- Limit overhead reaching
- No heavy gripping with trunk rotation
- Support arm during hand exercises
