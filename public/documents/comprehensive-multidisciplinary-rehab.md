## ðŸ“Š Assessment Framework

### Daily Self-Assessment Protocol

#### Morning Check (Before Rising)
**Position:** Supine in bed
**Process:**
1. **Breathing Pattern:**
   - Hand on chest, hand on belly
   - 5 natural breaths
   - Note: Chest dominant? Belly dominant? Balanced?

2. **Rib Mobility:**
   - Hands on lower ribs
   - Deep breath in
   - Feel for: Equal expansion? Left restricted? Right restricted?

3. **Pain Mapping:**
   - Mental scan from head to toe
   - Rate each area 0-10
   - Note: Sharp? Dull? Burning? Aching?

4. **Stiffness Assessment:**
   - Gentle limb movements
   - Note areas of resistance
   - Compare to yesterday

#### Movement Screen (After Rising)
**Perform gently, stop if pain >4/10**

1. **Standing Posture Check:**
   - Back against wall
   - Note gaps: Low back? Mid back? Neck?
   - Shoulder blade position

2. **Rotation Test:**
   - Arms crossed on chest
   - Turn left, turn right
   - Measure: Nose alignment with shoulder

3. **Side Bend Test:**
   - Hand slides down leg
   - Compare left vs right distance
   - Note: Pull or pinch sensation?

4. **Single Leg Stand:**
   - 30 seconds each leg
   - Note: Which is easier? Wobble pattern?

#### Evening Review
**Document in journal:**
- Best part of day physically
- Worst part of day physically
- Instability events count
- Activities that helped
- Activities that hurt

## ðŸ”„ Phase-Based Rehabilitation

### PHASE 1: Foundation (Months 12-15) - CURRENT

#### Goals
- Restore breathing patterns
- Reduce protective guarding
- Establish movement confidence
- Address foot-to-rib connection

#### Daily Minimums
1. **Breathing Practice:** 3x daily, 5 minutes
2. **Gentle Mobility:** 2x daily, 10 minutes
3. **Stability Work:** 1x daily, 10 minutes
4. **Walking:** 20-30 minutes total

#### Key Exercises

**1. Three-Part Breathing**
*Position:* Supine, knees bent
*Technique:*
- Part 1: Breathe into belly (5 breaths)
- Part 2: Breathe into lower ribs (5 breaths)
- Part 3: Breathe into upper chest (5 breaths)
- Part 4: Full wave breath combining all (10 breaths)
*Focus:* Smooth transitions, no forcing

**2. Pelvic Clock**
*Position:* Supine, knees bent
*Technique:*
- Imagine pelvis as clock face
- Tilt to 12:00 (toward head) and 6:00 (toward feet)
- Tilt to 3:00 (right) and 9:00 (left)
- Make small circles clockwise, then counter
*Purpose:* Restore lumbopelvic rhythm without rib stress

**3. Heel Slides with Breathing**
*Position:* Supine
*Technique:*
- Inhale: Slide one heel away slowly
- Exhale: Slide heel back
- Keep low back neutral
- Alternate legs
*Sets:* 10 per leg
*Integration:* Coordinates breathing with movement

**4. Standing Weight Shift**
*Position:* Standing, feet hip-width
*Technique:*
- Shift weight to right foot (70/30)
- Hold 5 seconds, breathing normally
- Shift to left foot (70/30)
- Hold 5 seconds
- Progress to 90/10 when stable
*Purpose:* Retrains balance and proprioception

### PHASE 2: Integration (Months 16-18)

#### Goals
- Increase functional range of motion
- Build endurance
- Add resistance training
- Return to more activities

#### Progressions
- Breathing: Add resistance (elastic band around ribs)
- Mobility: Increase range to 70%
- Stability: Add perturbations
- Walking: Increase to 45 minutes

#### New Additions
- Light resistance bands
- Modified yoga poses
- Pool therapy if available
- Gentle cycling

### PHASE 3: Performance (Months 19-24)

#### Goals
- Return to desired activities
- Build resilience
- Prevent re-injury
- Discharge from active care

#### Advanced Work
- Sport-specific training
- Full resistance training
- Dynamic movements
- Plyometric preparation

### Stress Management Techniques

#### Quick Calm (2 minutes)
1. **4-7-8 Breathing:**
   - Inhale 4 counts
   - Hold 7 counts
   - Exhale 8 counts
   - Repeat 4 cycles

#### Body Scan (10 minutes)
1. Start at toes, notice sensation
2. Move up slowly through each body part
3. Don't judge, just notice
4. End at crown of head
5. Three deep breaths to finish

#### Progressive Relaxation (15 minutes)
1. Tense muscle group 5 seconds
2. Release suddenly
3. Notice the contrast
4. Move through whole body
5. End with whole-body tension/release

### Sleep Optimization

#### Pre-Sleep Routine
**9:00 PM:** Screens off, dim lights
**9:15 PM:** Magnesium supplement

**Acceptable:** Supine

**Avoid:** Left side-lying initially
